Accessibility Matters

Designing digital and physical spaces that include everyone.

What is Accessibility?

Accessibility is the practice of making websites, apps, and environments usable for all people, including those with disabilities. It ensures that everyone can access information, complete tasks, and participate equally in digital and physical spaces.

Why Accessibility Matters

Accessibility is important because it removes barriers that prevent people from fully participating in everyday life. Good design helps everyone, not just people with disabilities.

  • Creates equal opportunities for all users.
  • Improves usability and user experience.
  • Supports independence and inclusion.
  • Makes technology more efficient and universal.

Accessibility Facts

Over 1 billion people worldwide live with some form of disability.

Many websites still fail basic accessibility standards today.

Accessible design benefits all users, not just those with disabilities.

Accessible Web Design Tips

Small design choices can make a big difference in accessibility.

  • Use descriptive alt text for images.
  • Ensure strong color contrast for readability.
  • Make all features keyboard accessible.
  • Provide captions for videos.
  • Use clear headings and simple navigation.
  • Design for mobile and responsive layouts.
