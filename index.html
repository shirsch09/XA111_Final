Why Accessibility Matters

Why designing digital and physical spaces that include everyone is important.

What is Accessibility?

Accessibility is the practice of making experiences open to all. It is how easily and effectively a product or service can be accessed and used. Good accessibility is designed for all types of people.

— A Web for Everyone

How it Helps

Accessibility removes barriers that prevent people from fully participating in everyday life.

  • - Creates equal opportunities for all all.
  • - Improves usability and user experience.
  • - Supports independence and inclusion.
  • - Makes technology more universal.

Accessibility Statistics

"Between 15% - 25% of the population lives with a disability."

— What is Digital Accessibility?

"70% of government, news, and e-commerce websites are inaccessible to individuals with visual impairments."

— Nucleus Research

"As few as 3% of people have access to the assistive technology they need."

— World Health Organization

Accessible Web Design Tips

Small changes in design can make websites easier for everyone to use.

  • Use descriptive alt text for images
  • Keep strong color contrast for readability
  • Make sure everything works with a keyboard
  • Add captions or transcripts for videos
  • Keep navigation simple and consistent
